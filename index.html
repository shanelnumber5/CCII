Who We Serve

The priority population includes students in General Education courses, especially when those courses serve as prerequisites for programs.

Priority Student Groups

  • Single parents
  • Part-time students
  • Returning and transfer students
  • Online-only students
  • First-generation learners
  • Adult learners

Student Realities

  • Place-bound and flexibility-dependent
  • Balancing work, family, and school
  • Often overcommitted
  • May feel disconnected from campus
  • Need clarity about next steps

GFC MSU Strengths

  • Faculty who understand student complexity
  • Advising and student services connections
  • Workforce and community relevance
  • Opportunities to embed career language in coursework

Student and Employer Voices

Student Perspective

  • “I don’t know how this applies to my career.”
  • “I’m balancing work, school, and life.”
  • “I don’t know what employers actually want.”
  • “I feel disconnected from campus.”
  • “It’s hard to know what comes next.”
  • “It’s a lot to figure out on my own.”

Employer Perspective

  • Students need to communicate, document, and show up professionally.
  • Employers hire for skills that are not always visible on a transcript.
  • Students may not know workplace expectations until they are already in the door.
  • The gap between classroom and workplace can be wider than students realize.

Action Plan for Thursday
