Project Overview

Students develop valuable workplace skills throughout their academic experiences, but those skills are not always visible to them. This tool supports a shared language between coursework, advising, career readiness, and student reflection.

Core idea: General Education helps students practice communication, critical thinking, collaboration, problem solving, information literacy, cultural awareness, and self-development.

        Reflection & Skill Articulation

        Translating academic experiences into career language must be intentional. Reflection, skill articulation, and transferable language development can help students, faculty, staff, and employer partners better recognize how learning connects to growth, career readiness, and evolving opportunities.

        Coursework often develops communication, leadership, collaboration, adaptability, problem-solving, and professional skills. Creating shared opportunities for reflection and skill translation can help make learning more visible, meaningful, and connected across the student journey.

        The Napkin Pitch

        What if every Gen Ed course, advising conversation, assignment, co-curricular experience, and campus interaction helped students build career-ready skills in real time?

        By translating learning outcomes and student experiences into workplace competencies, skill-based badges, reflective learning, and clear professional language, students can better understand the value of General Education and how every experience contributes to future success.

        The foundation of this work is institutional alignment through consistent collaboration, relationship building, and co-creating shared definitions of accountability, flexibility, ownership, and support for students navigating time poverty and complex life demands.

        Learning becomes more visible when institutions intentionally connect classroom experiences, reflection, career readiness, and student identity development.

        Action Plan

        Later

        • Align with career services and transfer preparation.
        • Create a sustainable shared-language model.
